# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors, maintainers, and leaders of the OpenSIN-Code project pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone—regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, accessible, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Encouraged Behaviors

The following behaviors are encouraged and expected:

- **Be Respectful and Inclusive** — Use welcoming and inclusive language. Respect differing viewpoints and experiences.
- **Be Collaborative** — Work together constructively. Share knowledge freely and help others learn.
- **Be Constructive** — Provide constructive feedback. Critique ideas, not people. Focus on improving the project.
- **Be Gracious in Disagreement** — Disagreements are natural. Disagree respectfully and seek common ground.
- **Show Empathy** — Be mindful of how your words and actions affect others. Assume positive intent.
- **Contribute Meaningfully** — Add value through code, documentation, design, testing, or community support.
- **Follow Technical Best Practices** — Write clean, maintainable code. Document your work. Test thoroughly.
- **Help Others Succeed** — Mentor newcomers. Share what you've learned. Celebrate others' contributions.

### Unacceptable Behaviors

The following behaviors are unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

- **Harassment** — Harassment includes offensive comments related to personal characteristics, deliberate intimidation, stalking, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- **Discrimination** — Discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic is prohibited.
- **Trolling and Personal Attacks** — Deliberate provocation, personal attacks, and inflammatory remarks are not allowed.
- **Publishing Private Information** — Sharing private information about others without explicit consent (doxing) is prohibited.
- **Unwelcome Advances** — Unwelcome advances, whether verbal, physical, or otherwise, are not acceptable.
- **Spam and Self-Promotion** — Unsolicited promotions, spam, or excessive self-promotion in community spaces is not allowed.
- **Deliberate Disruption** — Disrupting community discussions, flooding channels, or deliberately breaking functionality is prohibited.
- **Violence and Threats** — Threats of violence or encouraging harmful behavior are strictly forbidden.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. They will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines when determining consequences for violations:

### 1. Correction
**Consequence:** A private, written warning providing clarity about the nature of the violation and explaining why the behavior was inappropriate.

**Action:** Maintainer contacts the person privately to discuss the issue. No public disclosure unless the person continues the behavior.

### 2. Warning
**Consequence:** A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior.

**Action:** Written warning that specifies consequences. Further violations result in temporary suspension from community spaces.

### 3. Temporary Suspension
**Consequence:** A temporary ban from community participation for a specified period.

**Action:** Immediate suspension from GitHub issues, discussions, Discord/Slack, and other community channels. No public interaction during the suspension period.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Consequence:** Permanent removal from the community.

**Action:** Permanent ban from all community spaces. No possibility of reinstatement. All accounts associated with the person are banned.

### Appeal Process

Individuals affected by enforcement actions may appeal by contacting the maintainers at support@opensin.ai. All appeals will be reviewed within 14 days. Maintainers will provide a written explanation of their decision.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories and pull requests
- Issue trackers and discussion forums
- Social media channels and Discord/Slack
- Community events and meetups
- Any other platform where the OpenSIN-Code project is discussed

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the maintainers:

- **Email:** support@opensin.ai
- **GitHub Issues:** Use the "Report" label on issues
- **Security Issues:** See SECURITY.md for vulnerability reporting

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.
